Mechanisms
of Jim Crow.

Reference
Jim Crow operated through interlocking mechanisms — not one tool, but five working together. Most images show more than one. Your work is to identify which one is primary in the image you are analyzing, and to defend why.
1
Legal Segregation
Laws and statutes that mandated racial separation. Plessy v. Ferguson (1896) provided the constitutional cover. Codified in Jim Crow statutes across most of the country.
Look for: signage, designated facilities, ordinances, court rulings, posted rules
→ See which events targeted this mechanism
2
Economic Control
The ways the system extracted Black labor and excluded Black families from wealth-building. Sharecropping, debt peonage, employment discrimination, exclusion from federal programs (GI Bill, FHA, Social Security agricultural exemptions), redlining.
Look for: labor arrangements, housing finance, business ownership, federal program access, capital flows
→ See which events targeted this mechanism
3
Violence or the Threat of Violence
Lynching, riots, organized terror (Klan, white citizens' councils), police violence, mob attacks on Black families integrating white spaces. EJI documents 4,400+ racial terror lynchings between 1877 and 1950.
Look for: mobs, weapons, police presence (or absence), bodies, burned property, intimidation
→ See which events targeted this mechanism
4
Spatial / Architectural Separation
The physical and geographic enforcement of segregation. Walls, restrictive covenants in deeds, sundown towns, segregated facilities, residential redlining, separate neighborhoods.
Look for: walls, maps, neighborhood boundaries, deeds, signs marking territory, architecture of separation
→ See which events targeted this mechanism
5
Daily Life Under Segregation
The texture of life within the system — the choreography of survival, the Negro Motorist Green Book, the small bodily moments that show what it meant to live inside Jim Crow rather than just to be governed by it.
Look for: bodies in space, the small moment, navigation of the system, what is not said, the unposed
→ See which events targeted this mechanism
